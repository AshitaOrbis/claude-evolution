# European Genealogy Archives Reference

A comprehensive guide to free and accessible European genealogy resources.

## Pan-European Resources

### FamilySearch (familysearch.org)
- **Coverage**: Global, excellent European coverage
- **Cost**: Free (requires account)
- **Strengths**: Massive collection, continuous additions, community trees
- **Record Types**: Census, vital, church, immigration, military
- **Tips**: Check the wiki for country-specific guidance

### Geneanet (geneanet.org)
- **Coverage**: France-centric, Europe-wide
- **Cost**: Free basic, premium for some features
- **Strengths**: Collaborative trees, document archives
- **Record Types**: Family trees, notarial records, photos

### Matricula Online (matricula-online.eu)
- **Coverage**: Austria, Germany, Poland, Italy, Slovenia, Serbia, Luxembourg, Bosnia
- **Cost**: Free
- **Strengths**: Direct access to Catholic church register scans
- **Record Types**: Baptisms, marriages, deaths from parish registers

### Europeana (europeana.eu)
- **Coverage**: Pan-European cultural heritage
- **Cost**: Free
- **Strengths**: Digitized documents, photos, maps
- **Record Types**: Historical documents, photographs, maps

## Record Types by Era

### Pre-Civil Registration
- Church/parish registers (baptism, marriage, burial)
- Manorial records
- Tax lists
- Land records

### Civil Registration Era
- Birth, marriage, death certificates
- Census records (varies by country)
- Military records
- Immigration records

### 20th Century
- Above plus:
- Draft/conscription records
- Naturalization records
- Passenger manifests
- Social security/pension records

## Research Strategy by Region

### Germanic Areas (Germany, Austria, Switzerland)
1. Start with FamilySearch wiki for the region
2. Identify relevant church (Catholic vs Protestant)
3. Search Matricula (Catholic) or Archion (Protestant)
4. Check for indexed records on Compgen

### Eastern Europe (Poland, Czech, Hungary)
1. Determine which empire controlled the region
2. Search FamilySearch first
3. Use country-specific indexes (Geneteka, Actapublica)
4. Consider language of records

### Western Europe (France, Netherlands, Belgium)
1. Identify département/province
2. Search regional archives directly
3. Use aggregators (WieWasWie, Geneanet)
4. Check FamilySearch supplements

### Southern Europe (Italy, Spain, Portugal)
1. Check Antenati (Italy) or national archives
2. Church records often earliest source
3. Regional variation is significant

## Tips for Success

1. **Learn the geography** - Borders changed frequently
2. **Understand the calendar** - Julian vs Gregorian dates
3. **Know the naming conventions** - Patronymics, multiple surnames
4. **Recognize the script** - German Kurrent, Latin abbreviations
5. **Check multiple archives** - Records often duplicated
6. **Use translation tools** - But verify critical terms
7. **Document negative searches** - Know what you've already checked
