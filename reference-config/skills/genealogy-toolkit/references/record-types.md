# Genealogy Record Types Reference

A comprehensive guide to types of genealogical records and what information they contain.

## Vital Records

### Birth Records
**Information typically found**:
- Full name of child
- Date and place of birth
- Parents' names (father's full name, mother's maiden name)
- Parents' occupations and ages
- Sometimes: parents' birthplaces, witnesses

**Variants**:
- Civil birth registration
- Hospital birth records
- Delayed birth registration

**Tips**:
- Check both civil and church records
- Delayed registrations may have additional information
- Early records may only list father's name

---

### Marriage Records
**Information typically found**:
- Full names of both parties
- Ages or dates of birth
- Occupations
- Residences
- Parents' names
- Witnesses (often family members)
- Previous marriages

**Variants**:
- Civil marriage license/certificate
- Church marriage register
- Marriage banns (announcements)
- Marriage bonds

**Tips**:
- Witnesses are often relatives
- Banns may reveal residence history
- Check for both civil and church records

---

### Death Records
**Information typically found**:
- Full name of deceased
- Date and place of death
- Age at death
- Cause of death
- Place of birth (sometimes)
- Parents' names (sometimes)
- Spouse's name
- Occupation
- Burial location

**Variants**:
- Civil death registration
- Burial records
- Cemetery records
- Obituaries

**Tips**:
- Informant may be family member
- Information accuracy varies
- Cemetery records may supplement

---

## Census Records

### General Information Found
- Names of all household members
- Ages or birth years
- Relationships to head of household
- Occupations
- Birthplaces
- Sometimes: literacy, property ownership, immigration year

**Tips**:
- Check neighbors (FAN Club method)
- Ages often estimated
- Spelling varies dramatically
- Enumeration districts can help locate families

## Church Records

### Baptism/Christening Records
**Information typically found**:
- Child's name
- Date of baptism (not necessarily birth)
- Parents' names
- Godparents/sponsors (often relatives)
- Sometimes: parents' residence, occupations

**Tips**:
- Godparents are often grandparents, aunts, uncles
- Multiple baptisms if child sickly
- May predate civil registration by centuries

---

### Marriage Records (Church)
**Information typically found**:
- Names of couple
- Dates of banns and marriage
- Witnesses
- Parents' names (especially if minors)
- Residence/parish

**Tips**:
- Banns published 3+ weeks before wedding
- Check bride's parish and groom's parish
- Witnesses often family members

---

### Burial Records (Church)
**Information typically found**:
- Name of deceased
- Date of burial
- Age at death
- Sometimes: cause of death, relationship to others

**Tips**:
- Burial date is not death date
- May include more details than civil records
- Check for grave markers too

---

## Immigration and Emigration Records

### Passenger Manifests
**Information typically found**:
- Name
- Age
- Occupation
- Last residence
- Destination
- Traveling companions
- Port of departure/arrival
- Sometimes: birthplace, physical description

### Naturalization Records
**Information typically found**:
- Name (original and anglicized)
- Date and place of birth
- Date and port of arrival
- Current residence
- Occupation
- Spouse and children's names
- Physical description

### Border Crossing Records
**Information typically found**:
- Name
- Date of crossing
- Citizenship
- Destination
- Purpose of travel

**Tips**:
- Check both departure and arrival records
- Name spelling may differ significantly
- Groups often traveled together
- Earlier records have less detail

---

## Military Records

### Service Records
**Information typically found**:
- Full name
- Birth date and place
- Physical description
- Enlistment date and location
- Unit assignments
- Promotions and demotions
- Discharge information
- Next of kin

### Pension Records
**Information typically found**:
- All service record information plus:
- Marriage information
- Children's names and births
- Medical history
- Widow's information

### Draft/Conscription Records
**Information typically found**:
- Name
- Address
- Age/birth date
- Physical description
- Occupation
- Next of kin/dependents
- Citizenship status

**Tips**:
- Pension files are often most detailed
- Check all wars the person could have served in
- Consider both regular and militia service

---

## Land and Property Records

### Deeds
**Information typically found**:
- Names of buyer (grantee) and seller (grantor)
- Property description
- Sale price
- Date of transaction
- Witnesses

### Probate Records
**Information typically found**:
- Name of deceased
- Date of death
- List of heirs
- Property inventory
- Distribution of estate
- Executor/administrator

### Tax Records
**Information typically found**:
- Name of taxpayer
- Property description
- Assessed value
- Sometimes: occupation, household size

**Tips**:
- Neighbors on deed may be relatives
- Probate records often list all children
- Tax records annual, good for tracking residence

---

## Newspapers

### Obituaries
**Information typically found**:
- Full name and maiden name
- Birth and death dates
- Birthplace
- Parents' names
- Spouse(s)
- Children
- Occupation/life summary
- Burial location

### Other Newspaper Content
- Marriage announcements
- Birth announcements
- Legal notices (estate, land)
- Social columns
- Crime reports
- Business notices

**Tips**:
- Check local papers in both residence and birth locations
- Obituaries may appear days after death
- Legal notices often in small print

---

## Record Reliability

### Primary Sources (Original)
- Created at or near the time of event
- By someone with direct knowledge
- **Examples**: Birth certificate, marriage license

### Secondary Sources (Derivative)
- Created later from primary sources
- **Examples**: Published family histories, indexes

### Tertiary Sources (Compiled)
- Based on other derivative sources
- **Examples**: Online trees, encyclopedias

### Information Quality

| Quality | Description | Example |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| **Direct** | States the fact explicitly | "Born March 15, 1850" |
| **Indirect** | Fact must be inferred | Age "45" in 1895 census |
| **Negative** | Absence proves something | Not in 1880 census = likely died/emigrated |

---

## Research Priority Order

For a comprehensive search, check records in this order:

1. **Vital records** (birth, marriage, death)
2. **Census records** (all available years)
3. **Church records** (especially pre-civil registration)
4. **Probate/wills** (may list family members)
5. **Immigration records** (if applicable)
6. **Military records** (if applicable)
7. **Land records** (property ownership)
8. **Newspapers** (obituaries, announcements)
9. **City directories** (residence tracking)
10. **Tax records** (annual residence)
